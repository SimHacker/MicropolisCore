**Seymour Papert** (1928–2016) was a mathematician, computer scientist, and educator — a founder of the **MIT AI Lab** (with [**Marvin Minsky**](/pages/about/constructionist-education/marvin-minsky)) and of the **MIT Media Lab**. He studied with [**Jean Piaget**](/pages/about/constructionist-education/jean-piaget) in Geneva and extended Piaget's constructivism into **Constructionism**: the idea that people learn best by *building* personally meaningful things.

He co-created the **Logo** programming language and its **Turtle**, and his book ***Mindstorms*** (1980) reimagined how children could learn powerful ideas through "hard fun" in **microworlds**. (LEGO Mindstorms is named for it.) His ideas are the educational heart of why [**SimCity/Micropolis**](/pages/about/will-wright#educational-impact) matters as a learning medium.
