**Doreen (Gehry) Nelson** is an educator who created **Design-Based Learning (DBL)** (originally "City Building Education") — a methodology in which students learn any subject by **designing and building model cities and worlds**, then iterating ("backwards thinking"). She is **Professor Emerita** at Cal Poly Pomona (where she founded a DBL master's program in 1995) and has taught at the Art Center College of Design; the New York Times named her one of 30 top U.S. education innovators in 1991. She contributed to the original **Maxis SimCity** and wrote **teacher guides** for it. She is the younger sister of architect **Frank Gehry**.

Her city-building pedagogy draws on the broader [**constructionist**](/pages/about/constructionist-education) lineage of [**Seymour Papert**](/pages/about/constructionist-education/seymour-papert) and [**Jean Piaget**](/pages/about/constructionist-education/jean-piaget).
