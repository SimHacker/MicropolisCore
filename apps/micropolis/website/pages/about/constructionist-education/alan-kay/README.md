**Alan Kay** is a computing pioneer whose ideas shaped personal computing and learning. He conceived the **Dynabook** (the vision behind laptops and tablets as personal media for learning), led the team that built **Smalltalk** and much of the modern **graphical user interface** at **Xerox PARC**, and articulated **object-oriented programming** as message-passing between independent objects. Turing Award, 2003.

He is a fierce advocate for computing as a *medium for children to think with* (Squeak, Etoys, Viewpoints Research), and a noted critic of "black box" software — a critique the Micropolis project takes directly to heart in aiming to make the simulation open, visible, and modifiable.

His work carries [**Seymour Papert's**](/pages/about/constructionist-education/seymour-papert) and [**Jean Piaget's**](/pages/about/constructionist-education/jean-piaget) ideas about learning-by-building into the medium of the personal computer itself — the throughline at the heart of [**constructionist education**](/pages/about/constructionist-education).
